Validate an IBAN

Checks the country, length, format and mod-97 checksum. If a digit or two is unknown, type ? in its place (e.g. DE12500?0?170648489890) and the missing digits will be completed automatically.

Calculate an IBAN

Pick a country and enter the domestic bank details. Check digits — including national check digits (Belgium, France, Spain, Italy, Portugal) — are computed automatically.

How it works: every IBAN carries two check digits computed with the ISO 7064 mod-97-10 algorithm — all validation and calculation happens locally in your browser. A valid checksum means the IBAN is well-formed; it does not prove the account exists. BIC & bank names come from the free OpenIBAN directory service (AT, BE, CH, DE, LI, LU, NL) — that lookup is the only network request this page ever makes, and it sends nothing but the IBAN being checked.
